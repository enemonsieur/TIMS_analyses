## Input/Output Transformation

In this experiment, we applied TIMS pulses to a subject and recorded EEG. The stimulation protocol consisted of brief (≈9 ms) magnetic pulses delivered at a rate of 0.25 Hz (one pulse every 4 seconds). The underlying beat frequency generated by the temporal interference was 10 Hz, as confirmed by the `ground_truth` reference channel. The input transformation was thus:

- **Input:** TIMS pulses (0.25 Hz, 9 ms duration, 10 Hz beat frequency)
- **Output:** EEG response (31 channels, 1000 Hz sampling, 394.8 s duration)

The goal was to determine whether the 10 Hz neural oscillations in the EEG were synchronized to the stimulation pulses, and to characterize the nature of this input/output relationship.


---

## Analysis Pipeline & Key Functions

1. **Pulse Detection:**
   - Used the `stim` channel to detect pulse onsets via envelope thresholding (`np.abs(stim_data) > threshold`).
   - Found 83 pulses, each separated by exactly 4.00 s (ISI = 4.00 ± 0.0002 s).

2. **SSD (Spatio-Spectral Decomposition):**
   - Applied the `run_ssd_calibration_style()` function to extract spatial components maximizing 10 Hz power relative to broadband noise (8–12 Hz vs 3–22 Hz, with the signal band notched out in noise).
   - The SSD filter was applied to the EEG to obtain a continuous 10 Hz source.

3. **Epoching:**
   - Created epochs from -1 to +2 s around each pulse onset for the SSD source, ground_truth, stim, and a reference EEG channel (Cz).

4. **Metrics Computed:**
   - **PLV (Phase-Locking Value):** Quantifies the consistency of phase across trials at pulse onset. Calculated as $PLV = |\langle e^{i\phi} \rangle|$ where $\phi$ is the phase at pulse onset.
   - **ITPC (Inter-Trial Phase Coherence):** Measures phase concentration at each time point across trials.
   - **Rayleigh Test:** Statistical test for non-uniformity of phase distribution (significance of PLV).
   - **Envelope Modulation:** Mean amplitude of the 10 Hz component before and after the pulse.
   - **Eigenvalue Spectrum:** SSD eigenvalues indicate the SNR of extracted components (all < 1, meaning signal is weaker than noise).

---

## Findings & Interpretation

- **Pulse-locked PLV:** At pulse onset, PLV = 0.25 (Rayleigh p = 0.005), indicating significant phase concentration. This suggests the 10 Hz component is phase-reset or artifactually synchronized to the pulse.
- **ITPC:** Slight increase post-pulse (pre = 0.10, post = 0.12), but not dramatic.
- **Envelope Ratio:** No amplitude increase post-pulse (ratio = 0.94), indicating the pulse does not boost 10 Hz power.
- **SSD Eigenvalues:** All < 1 (best = 0.53), meaning the extracted 10 Hz component is not dominant over noise.
- **Figures:** Five figures were generated, including data overview, SSD decomposition, pulse-locked time courses, phase synchronization, and STIM vs baseline comparison.

**Conclusion:** The TIMS pulses induce significant phase concentration in the 10 Hz EEG component at pulse onset, but do not increase its amplitude. The SSD filter isolates the best spatial component, but the SNR is low. The observed phase-locking may reflect artifact or neural entrainment; further controls are needed.

**Functions Used:**
- `run_ssd_calibration_style`: SSD spatial filter extraction
- `np.abs`, `find_peaks`: Pulse detection
- `hilbert`, `np.angle`: Phase extraction
- PLV, ITPC, Rayleigh test: Synchronization metrics
