COMPARISON: PLV-based vs SNR-based Component Selection
=====================================================================

METHODOLOGY:
============

PLV-based (Original):
  Raw: Select channel with highest PLV vs GT (same channel for all intensities)
  SASS: Select SASS-cleaned channel with highest PLV vs GT per intensity
  SSD: Select SSD component with highest PLV vs GT per intensity

SNR-based (New):
  Raw: Select channel with highest SNR (signal-band / broadband) at 10%, lock it
  SASS: Select SASS-cleaned channel with highest SNR per intensity
  SSD: Select SSD component with highest SNR per intensity

NOTE: Raw channel locked at 10% in both methods (O1 in PLV-based, P8 in SNR-based).

==========================

CRITICAL INSIGHT:
=================

PLV remains HIGH (0.7-0.99) even when SNR is ZERO (raw at 40-50%).

This proves the component selection bias problem:
  - Both GT and STIM repeat at 12.45 Hz (by hardware design)
  - When SNR→0, component becomes pure noise + artifact
  - But that noise/artifact still LOCKS to the phase of STIM
  - PLV with GT stays high because GT and STIM are phase-locked
  → Selecting by PLV chooses components that lock to ARTIFACT PHASE
