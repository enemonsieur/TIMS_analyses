SNR vs PLV COMPARISON: The Selection Paradox Explained
================================================================================

HYPOTHESIS: SNR-based selection should correlate with PLV (phase locking).
REALITY: They often diverge, especially at high intensities.

  * Raw: SNR ≈ 0 but PLV ≈ 0.74–0.75 (artifact-contaminated but happens to stay in phase)
  * SSD: SNR ≈ 2–4 but PLV ≈ 0.18–0.50 (high SNR in the wrong component = random phase)

================================================================================
CONCLUSION: The Paradox
================================================================================

SNR-based selection works ONLY AFTER artifact removal (SASS path).

Without artifact removal, SNR selection is blind to:
  - Whether the 12.45 Hz power is signal or artifact
  - Whether that power is phase-coherent with ground truth
  - Spectral migration (target band power moving to 10 Hz)

At 40–50%:
  ✓ SASS: SNR selection succeeds because artifact is gone first
  ✗ SSD: SNR selection fails because it picks a component with high narrowband power
         but low phase coherence (comp2 instead of comp0)
  ✗ Raw: SNR metric collapses, but phase locking by accident still good


RECOMMENDATION:
Use ITPC (inter-trial phase coherence) or PLV as the selection metric for raw/SSD,
not SNR. SNR should only be used AFTER spatial filtering (like SASS) has removed
the artifact source.
